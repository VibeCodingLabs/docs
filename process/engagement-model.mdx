Every Sparkix engagement follows a consistent structure designed to give you clarity at each stage — from the first conversation to final handover and beyond. The process is built around transparency: you know what's being built, when it will be ready, and what it will cost before development begins. This page explains each stage and the pricing models available to you.

## Engagement stages

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initial consultation">
    Your engagement starts with a free AI Audit call. A Sparkix engineer reviews how your business operates today, identifies where AI can create the most value, and answers your questions about scope, timeline, and approach. There is no commitment required at this stage. See [The Sparkix AI Audit Process](/process/ai-audit) for a full breakdown of what the audit covers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Proposal and SOW">
    Based on the audit findings, Sparkix delivers a detailed proposal covering scope, timeline, team composition, and pricing. All engagements are governed by a Statement of Work (SOW), which defines deliverables, milestones, and acceptance criteria. Nothing moves forward until you have reviewed and signed the SOW.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Project kickoff">
    A dedicated team is assigned to your engagement. In the kickoff session, you establish the sprint cadence, communication channels, escalation paths, and access requirements. By the end of kickoff, your team knows exactly how work will flow and how progress will be reported.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Iterative delivery">
    Work is delivered in regular cycles with demos, milestone reviews, and structured feedback sessions. You have visibility into progress at all times and can provide input before each milestone is closed out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch and handover">
    At final delivery, Sparkix provides full documentation, knowledge transfer sessions, and transition support to ensure your team can operate and maintain the solution independently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ongoing support">
    After launch, you can opt into a post-launch support arrangement covering maintenance, model retraining as performance drifts, and incremental feature expansion as your needs evolve.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pricing models

Sparkix offers three commercial structures depending on the nature and scope of your engagement.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Fixed-price">
    The full scope, timeline, and price are agreed before work begins. Fixed-price engagements are well-suited to projects with clearly defined requirements and deliverables. You get cost certainty from day one, and Sparkix carries the risk of scope estimation.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Time & Materials">
    You are billed based on actual hours worked and resources consumed. Time & Materials engagements work best for projects where requirements are expected to evolve, or where ongoing development work is needed without a fixed end date. A monthly cap or budget ceiling can be set by agreement.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Milestone-based">
    Payment is tied to the delivery and acceptance of defined milestones rather than time or a fixed total. This model gives you the flexibility of incremental commitments while ensuring both parties are aligned on what constitutes successful delivery at each stage.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
