Sparkix builds enterprise software systems that are engineered to last — scalable under load, secure by design, and maintainable by your team after handoff. Whether you need a greenfield platform, a major system extension, or a modernization of legacy infrastructure, Sparkix works closely with your stakeholders to deliver software that meets both technical and business requirements.

## Development lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Requirements">
    Sparkix works with your product and engineering stakeholders to document functional and non-functional requirements, define success criteria, and agree on constraints — including compliance requirements, performance targets, and integration points.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Architecture">
    A senior Sparkix architect designs the system architecture, selecting appropriate patterns and technology choices for your scale, team, and operational environment. Architecture decisions are documented and reviewed with your team before development begins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Development">
    Sparkix engineers build the system in iterative delivery cycles, with regular demos and checkpoints. Code is written to production standards: reviewed, tested, and documented throughout — not as an afterthought at the end.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Testing">
    Every system goes through unit, integration, and end-to-end testing before release. For enterprise systems, Sparkix also conducts performance testing, security scanning, and — where required — penetration testing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deployment">
    Sparkix handles deployment to your target environment, including infrastructure provisioning and observability tooling so you have full visibility into system health from day one. Deployments are documented and repeatable so your team can operate and re-deploy independently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Support">
    Post-launch, Sparkix provides a transition period of knowledge transfer and stabilization support. Ongoing support and maintenance arrangements are available and scoped in the Service Agreement.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  SOC 2-aligned security practices are built into every phase of the Sparkix development process — not added at the end. This includes access control design, audit logging, encryption at rest and in transit, and regular security reviews throughout development.
</Note>
